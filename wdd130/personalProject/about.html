THE RICHMOND, VA TEMPLE:

This is the first temple to be built in the state of Virginia. Church members currently travel to Washington D.C. to participate in temple services, often enduring heavy stop-and-go traffic.

The preliminary plans proposed a two-story, 36,200-square-foot temple with a large steeple capped with an angel Moroni statue, an adjoining 16,200 square foot meetinghouse, a maintenance building, extensive landscaping, and a total of 240 parking spaces. An existing two-story home at 4151 Mountain Road will remain as a residence for the temple president and matron.

During his cosing remarks in the April 2018 General Conference, President Russell M. Nelson announced the Richmond, Virginia Temple. View it here.

There was a projected date for the completion of the temple, due to Covid19, it has been delayed. The Church has not updated the date yet.
